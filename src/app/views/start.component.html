The Grateful Dead Concert Explorer is a Web application that enables user to navigate and explore digital libraries of live music recordings and discover and share associated artifacts that are dynamically linked within the application. It aims to demonstrate how Semantic Audio and Linked Data technologies can produce an improved user experience for browsing and exploring live music collections, by collecting information from a variety of different Web sources and amploying Music Information Retrieval (MIR) techniques.

This prototype demonstrator focusses on the concerts of the Grateful Dead, motivated by the ongoing interest in detailed descriptions of their performances, evidenced by the large amount of information available on the Web detailing various aspects of those events. The application links the large number of concert recordings by the Grateful Dead available in the Internet Archive with audio analysis data and retrieves additional information and artifacts (e.g. band lineup, photos, scans of tickets and posters, reviews) from existing Web sources, to explore and visualise the collection. The project demonstrates mechanisms for musicians and the music industry to provide services for consumers to enjoy live music performances recognizing the social and cultural value of live music and fan communities.

This is an ongoing project in its early stages. The technologies exploited in this work that were developed in the EPSRC FAST IMPaCt project include:

  • Ontological data models for the representation of performed music as cultural artefacts, and its linkage to associated non-musical artefacts
  • Music information retrieval and processing algorithms for the automatic generation of playlists and audio mixes based on user-selected criteria
  • Music-structure analysis for the comparison of performances
  • Audio alignment of different user-generated recordings of live-music performances

The main functions of the current version are described in the following.

Map View

The Map View shows the world map with markers indicating the locations of concerts. Clicking on a marker opens a menu with links to the pages for the venue (Venue View), and to the pages for the concerts at that location (Show View). While by default all concert locations are displayed, specific tours can be seleceted from the pull down menu on the top left. When selecting a tour, only the concerts from the tour are displayed on the map and connected by arrows indicating the order of concerts. The map also has a seperate search function covering the concerts on the map.

Show View

The Show View shows information about a specific concert, including the location, venue, the setlist, the lineup and the additional recordings available in the archive. The button next to the recording IDs opens a dialogue to either go to the Recording View for the selected recording, or add all songs of the concert recording to the playlist of the audio player. One can navigate to other pages by clicking on any item, for instance, clicking on the venue opens the Venue View for the respective venue. Clicking on the button next to a song opens a dialogue to either go to the Song View or add the song to the playlist of the audio player. Clicking on photos or other artifacts either opens a higher resolution image or opens the source page at the Grateful Dead Archive Online (GDAO) in a new tab.

Song View

The Song View shows information about a selected song. This includes the composers at the top of the page and the number of performances and recordings available. Clicking on an item in the list of shows opens the Show View. The button next to the location opens a dialogue which additionally lets the user add the song from the resepctive show to the playlist of the audio player. The dialogue shows all available recordings from the selected concert.

Location View

The Location View shows information about a city where concerts were held. The list of shows provides links to the Show View for all shows at that location.

Venue View

The Venue View shows information about a venue where concerts were held. The list of shows provides links to the Show View for all shows at that venue.

Recording View

The Recording View shows information about a specific recording from the Live Music Archive at the Internet Archive. The information includes source and lineage information of the recording,a s well as the list of songs. Clicking on the button next to a song opens a dialogue to add the seleted song to the playlist of the audio player.

Audio Player

The audio player at the bottom of the page streams audio directly from the Live Music Archive. Songs can be added to the playlist from the Web application where songs are listed, for instance in the Show View, Recording View or Song View. In addition to the standard functions (play, stop, volume, etc.), the rightmost button with the eye symbol expands/hides the waveform view and playlist.

Additional Functions

Not all fucntions of the demo application are listed here, and there are more functions planned to be implemented in the near future. For instance, the automatic generation of playlist mixes based on user-defined parameters and audio feature analysis results.
